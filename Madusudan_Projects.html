E.S Palm


Project: Palm Pilot Family
Product: E.S Palm
Course: Introduction to Engineering (IED)
Duration: March 19 to April 8
Click Here For Project Powerpoint

Objective:

To build a Palm Pilot similar to the original one. The new product should have the look and feel of the original pilot, including the use of a stylus for graphical input. They should take advantage of the latest technology, including full-color touchscreens, Bluetooth connectivity to interact with and augment cell phone apps, and a built-in NFC antenna for data transfer. Our E.S Palm consists of all of these characteristics and still manages to incorparates new and high-demand features that have the potential to gain a large consumer base.

Description:

For this project, my group and me were given certain constraints, a rubric, and some parts that were optional to use. My role was to model the back camera, screen, card-holder, and to create a marketing presentation. Our main goal was to engineer a palm pilot that would carry daily essential needs with a touch of security. After discussing and researching past Palm Pilots we came up with a few features that would be more convenient for people. Our E.S Palm features a 4k DSLR camera, Intel I9 processor for fast computing, a Bluetooth module to track personal belongings, and a card holder that can be only unlocked with voice recognition. After modeling our design, we made a product presentation to sell our idea.Throughout the construction of our E.S Palm device, we suffered from dificult problems such as wrong dimensions and measurements. This messed up the overall design of our palm pilot, but we were able to tackle this by comparing measurements within the group efficently and adapting with what we had. Overall, my group and I learned a lot about selling our product and designing certain principles that can attract a mass audience.I was also able to improve my management qualities by keep the group motivated and focused on what they needed to accomplish.

The Adventures of Mr.Brown's Classroom


Project: Python Escape Room Challenge
Course: AP Computer Science Principles (AP CSP)
Duration: January 22 to January 30
Click Here For Project Documentation

Objective:

To create an Escape Room game that utilized Python as its main language. It was required that we follow the given rubric and criteria so that we could create our project based on the guidelines. In addition, we were also asked to use a flowchart to brainstorm and organize our ideas and additionally, to document all our code by using google docs and doc-strings within the code.

Description:

For this challenge, my partner and I decided to make our escape room project based off the theme of our class teacher, which would be Mr. Brown. To do this, we started a brainstorm session which highlighted key things that stood out in our teacher whether it meant his motto, quotes, or type of assignments he would always assign to us. In order to do this, we had to utilize a flowchart where we explained our decisions in if-else type statements and used our brainstormed ideas. Whatever was on our flowchart, we implemented in our python code in a more detailed way which would appeal to the user that would be playing our escape room game. My role in this project was to help code the if-else statements and also take care of the documentation. Regarding the coding aspects, we had to define functions as this would be more faster and efficient instead of typing out everything. As it seemed pretty easy at first, we struggled over problems that related to our questions and answers. For example, at times the code would prompt the wrong question at times and also deem right answers as wrong. To fix this, my partner and I would try testing different parts of the code to analyze which parts were not working properly. We took advantage of our leadership skills to rightfully direct us to what we needed to do and made sure that we had an abundance of communication. This led to us creating a successful project.
